Audio Capture Spike — Risk 1

Validates that getDisplayMedia({ audio: true }) reliably captures Google Meet tab audio, resamples to 16 kHz mono PCM, and is suitable for streaming to Cloud STT V2 (Chirp 3). Pre-Sprint 1 derisking per ADR 0006 (to be written based on results).

Browser support detection

Step-by-step instructions

1
Open meet.google.com in a separate tab. Join a test meeting (you can be alone in it).
2
Come back to this tab. Click Start capture.
3
In the picker, choose Chrome Tab, select your Meet tab, and tick “Share tab audio”. (Critical — without this checkbox, no audio is captured.)
4
Speak into your mic in the Meet tab. The level meter and PCM stats below should react.
5
Watch for dropouts when you mute/unmute Meet. Note any anomalies in the event log.

What to record in the ADR

  • Per OS+browser combo: did tab audio work? Did system audio work?
  • What happens when the user mutes Meet locally — does the captured stream go silent or does the rep voice get dropped?
  • Any audio cutouts, glitches, or sample-rate mismatches.
  • Permissions UX — how confused was the user during the picker step?
  • Resampler CPU cost (open DevTools Performance, check the AudioWorklet thread).
